Knockoff Supes

Other "heroes." Capes, powers, a little fight in them. None of it adds up to anything here.

Vought Tower

The gold spire at the dead center of the skyline, with a peaked glass roof up top — the tallest thing standing, and where the game opens with you bursting up through it. Hold the laser on the glass long enough and it shatters on its own, no need to bring the whole tower down first. Level the tower itself and the city eventually puts it back up.

03

The Bosses

Five of them, each on their own independent timer, each with a real gimmick worth learning. They come back a while after you kill them, so there's always another one coming.

Boss

Starlight

She used to stand next to me and smile for the cameras like she'd earned it. Now she just runs. I like her better this way.

Actively jukes out of your laser's path — catch her still or don't bother.
Boss

A-Train

Fast doesn't mean untouchable. It just means I get to enjoy watching him figure that out.

Long charging sprints across rooftops and street — telegraphed, but closes distance fast.
Boss

Queen Maeve

She grabbed me once. Thought that made her strong. I let her believe that, for a while.

Braces mirror-bright under sustained fire, then lunges in for a grapple.
Boss

The Deep

He talks to fish. He hides under the waves and thinks that's a kingdom. It's not, but the fish do listen.

Rides a dolphin into his leap and charge attacks — shoot the dolphin out from under him.
Boss

Lamplighter

He set people on fire for a paycheck and called it clean-up. I don't need a paycheck, and I'm not going to call it anything.

Close-range dragon-breath cone, ranged fireballs immune to your laser, and dodges almost as well as Starlight.
